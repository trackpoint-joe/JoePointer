My Perspective

Tri-State's AI Opportunity

The Insight

Tri-State has deep operational expertise and decades of institutional knowledge about how to generate and deliver power. The opportunity is taking that knowledge, combining it with AI capabilities, and designing solutions your teams actually adopt and use to make better decisions.

That's where most AI initiatives stall. Not in the technology, but in the adoption. And closing adoption gaps is what I've done my entire career.

Three Areas Worth Exploring First

Based on our conversation and my research, these are the areas I'd want to dig into during the first 30 days:

  1. Workforce AI Enablement: I'd want to identify where teams are spending time on repetitive, manual work and show them what's actually possible with the tools they already have access to. When people see AI solve a problem they deal with every day, adoption takes care of itself. This is the baseline for everything else.
  2. Compliance and Document Intelligence: An AI assistant that helps teams quickly find answers across the policies, standards, and regulatory documents they work with every day. This is consistently one of the fastest wins in any organization because it cuts the time people spend hunting for information that already exists.
  3. Decision Intelligence: In every organization I've worked in, there are decisions being made every day based on experience and gut feel because better information isn't easily accessible. I'd want to sit with teams across the business and understand where those gaps are. That's where AI creates the most tangible value, helping people make better decisions with better information.

Action Plan

What I'd Do in the First 90 Days

This is a starting point, not a final plan. I'd want to shape this together with you based on your priorities and what I learn in the first few weeks. But it gives a sense of how I think about the work and the pace I'd want to move.

Days 1–30

Listen, Learn, and Deliver a Quick Win

  • Stakeholder listening tour across every business unit to understand real problems, not pitch AI
  • Map the data landscape and regulatory boundaries
  • Identify and deliver a first quick win within the first few weeks. I built a working prototype in a few hours for the interview. I'd want to put something useful in front of a real team just as fast.
  • Deliver an "AI Landscape Assessment" brief to you with prioritized opportunities

Days 31–60

Establish Governance and Expand

  • Stand up the AI Council with cross-functional membership, a tiered risk framework, and clear decision paths
  • Take the early win and expand it based on feedback and adoption
  • Begin AI literacy program designed around people's actual jobs, not abstract concepts
  • Evaluate build/buy/blend options for AI platform stack

Days 61–90

Scale What's Working, Present the Roadmap

  • Document results into a repeatable adoption playbook
  • Build champion network inside business units
  • Present enterprise AI roadmap to the leadership team, grounded in real results, not theory
  • Pipeline the next 2-3 initiatives based on demand and impact
I've structured this plan to establish governance and culture first, before scaling. Getting the foundation right in the first 60 days means everything after it moves faster. And because Tri-State operates in a highly regulated environment, building compliance awareness into the AI program from day one isn't optional. It's the architecture.
